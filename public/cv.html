CIVIL SERVANT (2010–2015)

I held five posts at the UK Department of Energy & Climate Change:

Deputy Director, Analysis

Reporting to the Chief Economist, I acted as an analytic troubleshooter. In particular, I wrote code to help the team move from a traditional approach of considering a few long-range energy scenarios to being able to look for the common features, level of uncertainty and interesting exceptions amongst thousands of automatically generated scenarios.

Deputy Director, International Climate Change

I ran a nine-person team and £2 million programme providing advice to other governments on how to think about the future of their energy systems. The team won a Civil Service Award, jointly with the Foreign Office, for their approach, which relied on tools, techniques and code I co-developed and open-sourced.

Due to the departure of a colleague I also briefly & simultaneously managed another team during a challenging period. I was responsible for an additional thirty staff and £200 million of overseas development aid.

Deputy Director, Strategy

I ran a twelve-person team that considered what needed to be done to meet the department's long-term targets. The team won a Civil Service Award for its ground-breaking approach which relied, in part, on code I developed.

Grade 6, Head of Engineering Team

I formed and ran the Department of Energy & Climate Change's first central engineering team. This team of ten spanned the engineering disciplines and provided technical advice to the department on carbon capture & storage, bioenergy sustainability, nuclear research, spending to support innovation, the potential for energy demand reduction and the challenges of renewables deployment.

Grade 7, Assistant to the Chief Scientific Advisor

I was recruited to the UK Department of Energy & Climate Change by David MacKay, the Chief Scientific Advisor, to work in his private office. I supported him with counsel and rapid analysis, helped track initiatives inside and outside of the department and represented him when he was unavailable.

STRATEGY MANAGER (2007–2010)

I was appointed as a strategy associate and then promoted to strategy manager at the Carbon Trust. I managed internal and external staff to create well-received publications on climate change topics and to help the CEO and COO to define elements of the Carbon Trust’s organisation and strategy.
