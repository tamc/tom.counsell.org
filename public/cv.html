CIVIL SERVANT (2010–2015)

Deputy Director, Analysis

Reporting to the Chief Economist, I acted as an analytic trouble shooter. Deputy Director, Strategy

Grade 6, Head of Engineering Team

Grade 7, Assistant to the Chief Scientific Advisor

STRATEGY MANAGER (2007–2010)

I was appointed as a strategy associate and then promoted to strategy manager at the Carbon Trust. I managed internal and external staff to create well received publications on climate change topics (four on the EU ETS with Michael Grub, one on the impact of tackling climate change on shareholder value and one on the scale of emissions triggered abroad by UK consumption of goods) and to help the CEO and COO to define elements of the Carbon Trust’s organisation and strategy (for example, how should the Carbon Trust support other governments in setting up Carbon Trust-like organisations? ENGINEERING RESEARCHER (2003–2007)

I completed a PhD under the supervision of Dr Julian Allwood at the Department of Engineering at the University of Cambridge. I won funding for my research on the basis of my proposal and academic background. My research considered whether it would be possible to remove print from paper in a way that would allow used sheets to be fed back into a printer or photocopier and immediately reused. During this research I explored three distinct fields: solvents, in collaboration with the Department of Chemistry; lasers, in collaboration with the Bavarian Laser Centre and the Centre for Industrial Photonics; and high speed wear, in collaboration with the Production Processes Group. I published four peer reviewed journal articles.

ENGINEERING TEACHER (2003–2007)

I marked essays. I prepared and delivered lectures on, for example, industrial sustainability, the Toyota Production System, and process mapping.
