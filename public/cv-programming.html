Projects delivered

2015–Today

Tapestry

A website, Android app and iOS app that children's nurseries use to plan, record and communicate how their charges develop. More than 17 000 schools and nurseries pay for the service.

It contains structured and unstructured data, pictures and videos relating to more than 900 000 children and has more than 1 000 000 active users. Written in PHP, Javascript, Java and Swift and hosted on AWS.

I managed the team of six software engineers; I managed bringing app development in-house; managed the migration to the cloud; managed the process of complying with the GDPR; developed tooling and processing to cope with growth in demand and in team-size; designed a substantial part of the architecture and contributed code and to all parts of the site and apps.

Key features:

  • A requirement for high security and reliability.
  • A complex legacy, multi-author codebase.
  • Smooth scaling to cope with more than 4 times the load without customer's noticing.
  • Daily deployment of improvements, without downtime.
2018

Net Zero 2050

An interactive site that allows people to explore the future of energy in the EU: what will we use it for? where will we get it from? A massively more complex extension of the UK model I developed 2010-2015 and described below. Written in Javascript (using jD3js), Go, Excel and C. Deployed on serverless infrastructure. I developed the architecture and tooling and provided coaching, trouble-shooting and support to the multi-disciplinary team.

Key features:

  • Based on the automatic translation of a very complex, multi-author, very large Excel model into C interfaced with Go.
  • Running on an auto-scaling serverless infrastructure to support highly variable peak loads at the lowest possible cost and maintenance overhead.
2015

The Global Food and Water System

An interactive site developed for an Australian group that allows people to explore whether 14 countries will have enough food and water under a range of possible futures. Written in Javascript (using D3js), Ruby (using Sinatra), Excel and C. I improved the model and developed the interactive site.

Key features:

  • A well structured Excel spreadsheet that contains the model and assumptions.
  • Code that takes an Excel spreadsheet and turns it into an equivalent model written in C.
  • Code to interface between the C library and the javascript in the browser.
  • Bespoke javascript controls and charts.

An interactive site that allows people to explore the future of energy in the UK: what will we use it for? where will we get it from? Written in Javascript (using jQuery, Raphael & D3js), Ruby (using Sinatra), Excel and C. I ran the 10 person multi-disciplinary team, developed the interactive site and some of the model.
